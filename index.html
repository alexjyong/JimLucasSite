The Key To It All; The Endocannabinoid System

In each and every one of us, exists the endocannabinoid system. In a nutshell, the ECS helps maintain homeostasis in the body by regulating celluar inflammatory responses, neuron firing and many other processes. A misbalance in this system is what causes many issues that can be treated by medical cannabis. Cannabis' compounds help by replacing missing endocannabinoids between cellular communication.


Examples of the ECS in action!

According to Leafly, one example of the ECS coming into play during a misbalance involves neurons firing signals to each other in order to process bodily functions. However, it is possible for neurons to get in a faulty state in which they send too many signals to another one. Endocannabinoids come into play when a neuron receiving too many signals from another neuron will send back endocannabinoids to it, instructing it to quiet down.


Quieting faulty neurons however, isn't the only role that endocannabinoids play. Regulating the inflammatory response is also done by our ECS. During an infection, our bodies cause inflammation in order to help fight off whatever is attacking us. However, it is possible for the inflammation to be excessive, and lead to the destruction of healthy cells. Our ECS comes into play by regulating the inflammatory agents in the body; preventing an inbalanced state with the inflammatory response. It is because of this is why folks with pain issues caused by inflammation find relief from medical cannabis.

FAQs


  • Why do you think that medical cannabis would be good for the state?

    Besides providing scientifically proven medicine to our most ill residents, medicial cannabis will help provide an alternative to problematic painkillers, an economic boom for our state, and allow our law enforcement to focus on other issues in our state, instead of spending resources enforcing laws based on misguided information.

  • Wait, don't you have to smoke medical cannabis? How is smoking anything good for you?

    The idea that cannabis can only be consumed via smoking is based on lack of information of modern cannabis delivery methods. Today, there exist vaporizers, edibles, tinitures, salves and many other products. None of these require combustion and are clean! An excellent handout written by IndyCann on this can be read here.

  • What about intoxicated drivers? I've heard reports about an increase of "stoned" drivers in legal states.

    While any number of intoxicated drivers is too many, many of these reports are based upon misleading information. While it is true that the number of drivers involved in accidents in legal states tested positive for cannabis, this isn't the whole picture. We only know that they consumed cannabis at some point in the past. This does NOT prove intoxication at the time of the crash. Many tests are done with urine or blood tests, which while these work for alcohol intoxication, since cannabis in a non-psychoactive state can exist in the body after consumption for at least a week, this doesn't prove anything.


  • I'm a gun owner, and could benefit from medical cannabis. Unfortunately, I've heard reports of people in medical states having their right to bear arms threatened because of federal law. How do you plan to handle that?

    While it was shunted to a summer study, HB1106 contained a provision that would criminalize any state official from aiding a federal law enforcement officer in the enforcement of federal law with respect to cannabis. This would include the right to own a firearm. States have NO DUTY to enforce federal law!

  • What's wrong with just sticking with CBD? We just legalized that!

    While CBD extract is great for some folks, and is a step in the right direction, it's simply not enough for other folks. The cannabis plant contains quite a number of compounds that are needed in order to help treat an ailment. CBD is just one of those compounds, and for some folks, is insufficent. Whole plant medicine is the right way to help this.

  • I hear a lot of talk about treating the opioid crisis with cannabis. This sounds great and all, but aren't we just substituting one drug for another?

    Statements like this assume that opioids and cannabis are somehow equilvaent. This is most certainly not the case! For example, while everyday it seems like there is a new opioid overdose, not only have legal states seen a reduction in opioid deaths but throughout history, there has been no overdoses from cannabis. Even the DEA has admitted this!
